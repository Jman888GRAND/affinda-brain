# Cover Letter — Affinda Winter Internship 2026

Submitted April 22, 2026. Written for [[Affinda Winter Internship 2026]].

---

"There are basically two ways to know you have a future. One, you have some vocational training. Or two, you're neurodivergent." — Alex Karp on TBPN, March 2026

I am a final year PPE student at Monash University, specialising in economic analysis. I am an unconventional applicant for this role as my academic performance has been... inconsistent. I have struggled with ADHD throughout uni which made unengaging tasks feel impossible to submit on time, and I often felt too ashamed to ask for help. As an economics student, I had to learn how to use AI tools on my own. What economics did give me was a way of thinking: systems, incentives, abstraction, first principles. The kind of thinking that turns a document parsing API into a knowledge graph. This was when I realised that in my adult life, I felt like an artist without the means to create. AI is the canvas I didn't know I was searching for.

So let me show you today's creation: I built AffindaBrain in a single session using your API. It's a career intelligence knowledge graph that parses resumes and job descriptions into an interconnected markdown wiki. Candidates, skills, organisations, and match analyses are all linked in one database. This project is live at: https://jman888grand.github.io/affinda-brain and I made a short walkthrough here: https://www.loom.com/share/5d87454c83d049288c185e8b614456fc

I built this on Wednesday April 22, the same day I was sent the job listing. When I saw applications received before April 22 may be given preference, I knew there was no time to waste. I built this without prior API experience because I find the problem genuinely interesting and I learn fastest by building. I also compete in the highest division of Men's volleyball in the state, run the boys volleyball program at [[Huntingtower School]], and have professional experience with [[Lysander Consulting]]. I know I'm not a traditional candidate, but in the new age of AI, I don't think that's the problem it used to be.
